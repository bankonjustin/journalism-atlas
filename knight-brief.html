01 — The Problem

Legacy journalism is contracting. Creator journalism is growing. There's no infrastructure connecting them.

The information ecosystem is rapidly shifting from institution-centered to individual-centered. The shift Maribel Wadsworth named at Knight Media Forum — from institutional to individual journalism — is already underway. Atlas is built for what comes next. But the infrastructure — discovery, standards, partnerships, funding pathways — need to move faster to match.

Washington Post

Halved its local staff in Q1 2026. Baltimore, Pittsburgh, New Orleans: legacy metro newsrooms — the papers and TV stations that defined local coverage for generations — are shrinking or gone entirely.

Philadelphia

40+ independent creators are covering Philadelphia right now — newsletters, YouTube, TikTok, podcasts. Lenfest has no systematic way to find them. Neither does anyone else.

Every Knight City

Has creators doing real civic journalism. None have discovery infrastructure, credibility standards, or partnership frameworks. Program directors have no visibility into who they are.

Press Forward ($150M+)

44 chapters across 34 states need to identify and fund credible independent creators locally, at speed. No systematic tool to do this currently exists.

Gannett & Hearst

The two largest owners of local news brands in America continue contracting, pulling reporters from communities and leaving behind "ghost newsrooms" — buildings with mastheads and no journalists.

The single most important framing

Atlas is not asking Knight to add a new bet. We're asking Knight to add the infrastructure layer that allows its existing bets — Press Forward, Knight Cities, Knight Chairs, portfolio grantees — to operate deep within the creator economy. This is infrastructure for Knight's portfolio. Not a grant program. Not a newsroom. The connective tissue that makes the whole portfolio more effective.

Minnesota: The Proof Point

During the ICE raids in January 2026, we deployed our spidering methodology and identified 50 independent creators covering local news within 24 hours — not weeks. These creators were immediately positionable for grant consideration, platform partnerships, and audience discovery.

This is rapid-response journalism infrastructure. It's city-agnostic and replicable anywhere Knight has a presence. The methodology is documented and public.

This is deliverable for Philadelphia, Charlotte, Detroit, and every other Knight resident city in 90 days each — if we can engage in the right partnerships.
