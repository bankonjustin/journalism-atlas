Why this partnership matters

The Atlas launched in February 2026 with 1,100+ US creator-journalists. Our international expansion is built on a foundation that would take years to replicate independently: ICFJ's database of 8,000+ media entities across 45 countries, accumulated through decades of fellowship programs, training initiatives, and the global Project Oasis creator mapping effort.

ICFJ's regional expertise provides what no algorithm can — cultural context, in-country credibility assessment, and access to creators who don't surface in US-centric discovery tools. Together, we're building a map of independent journalism that reflects the full global ecosystem.

Creators featured on the Atlas meet our independent creator-journalist criteria: primarily solo or small-team operations, direct audience distribution, original reporting or editorial voice. ICFJ's trust framework (green/yellow/red) informs our vetting. All green-rated creators are eligible for Atlas inclusion.

About ICFJ

The International Center for Journalists (ICFJ) is one of the world's leading journalism support organizations, with more than three decades of experience training and supporting journalists in 180+ countries. ICFJ's Knight Fellowships, awards programs, and Project Oasis initiative represent some of the most rigorous credentialing in global journalism.
