Two spidering sessions using the AT Protocol public API surfaced the shape of the independent journalism ecosystem on Bluesky — who's there, at what scale, in what beats, and how they cluster. The core finding: the Bluesky migration from Twitter isn't random. It's beat-driven. Health, tech, media criticism, national security, and climate creators moved as communities — and they follow each other. That network topology is the product. Atlas has the corpus to name it. TrustFnd has the infrastructure to monetize it. The bundle concept maps directly onto the clusters that already exist.

How We Spidered & What's Left

AT Protocol  ·  2 sessions  ·  open methodology

What worked

searchActors — no auth required. Primary discovery method. Single-concept queries work; multi-word specialty searches fail silently. Best queries: "investigative reporter," "independent journalist," "climate newsletter."
getFollows graph mining — seeded from Judd Legum, Erin Reed, Amy Westervelt, Jason Koebler, Kim Kelly, David M. Perry, Aaron Rupar (S1) then Michael Hobbes and Garrett M. Graff (S2). Each yields 100 follows; filtered by bio signal + follower threshold.
getProfiles (batch) — 25 actors per call. Used for bulk follower count retrieval after candidate identification. Fast and reliable across both sessions.
Named-seed lookups (S2) — Direct handle resolution for known food/culture/science names (Soleil Ho, Patrick Wyman, Ed Yong). Essential for beats where query searches return empty arrays. Ed Yong's getFollows then surfaced an entire health/science journalism cluster.

What was blocked

searchPosts — 403 without auth. This is the key missing tool. Post-level search would unlock starter pack discovery, mentions, and thread-level signal. TrustFnd's authenticated Bluesky access is the moat here.
blueskystarterpack.com — 403. Web-accessible directory of curated packs blocked to unauthenticated requests across both sessions.
Austin Graff as DC network seed. Bluesky account essentially inactive — 37 followers, follows only 4 accounts. Not a usable seed. Alternative: Dan Froomkin getFollows (80K, DC-based, already in Atlas).
