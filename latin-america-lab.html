The landscape

Latin American creator-journalism operates under conditions unlike most of the world. In Venezuela, Cuba, and Nicaragua, independent journalism has been forced into exile — creators like El Pitazo's César Batiz and El Toque's Alejandro Ulloa Garcia reach audiences from Spain and Colombia while covering their home countries in real time. Press freedom is not abstract here; it's the operating condition.

Across the rest of the region, a distinct pattern has emerged: veteran journalists leaving legacy TV and print to build direct audience relationships through YouTube, podcast, and newsletter. The trust deficit with institutional media has created a genuine opening for creator-led voices. Brazil, Colombia, and Chile are the most active markets. The Lusophone and Hispanophone spheres are distinct ecosystems requiring separate navigation.

Exile journalism
Venezuela, Cuba, Nicaragua. Creators operating from abroad but building audiences at home through VPNs and diaspora networks. High risk, high credibility.
Video-native voices
YouTube-first journalism is more dominant here than in the US ecosystem. Creators like La Pulla (Colombia) and MyNews (Brazil) have built mass audiences through explainer-style long-form video.
Nonprofit independence
Many LatAm creators operate under nonprofit or NGO structures, funded by international journalism grants. This is different from the US subscription-first model but no less independent.

How this list was built

This lab draws from ICFJ's global creator research database, which encompasses 8,000+ media entities across 45 countries. The Latin America list reflects research conducted through early 2026, including desk research, creator vetting by ICFJ's regional specialists, and Atlas editorial review.

Criteria for inclusion: original reporting or editorial voice; direct audience distribution (not primarily institutional); primarily solo or small team (under ~20 staff). Trust ratings (green/yellow) follow ICFJ's framework: green indicates transparency, published corrections policy, and clear editorial standards.

This is a living list. New creators are added as vetting is completed.
