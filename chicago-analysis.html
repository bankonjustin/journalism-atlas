Annual Revenue Distribution

Key Insight: Revenue is bimodal — a cluster at $5K–15K (emerging/subsidized creators) and a cluster at $75K–100K+ (established full-timers). The middle is thin, suggesting a “sustainability gap” that creators need to bridge.

Recommendations for Press Forward Chicago Grant Strategy

Based on survey findings (n=9, ongoing). Preliminary — will be refined with additional data, focus groups, and full landscape analysis.

1. Design for a Spectrum, Not a Type

Respondents range from $5K side projects to $100K+ full-time operations. Grant criteria should accommodate different stages. Consider tiered grants: smaller ($5K–10K) for early-stage creators needing runway, larger ($15K–25K) for established creators needing scaling support.

2. Don’t Require “Journalist” Identity

3 of 9 respondents don’t identify as journalists, yet they’re producing journalism. RFP language should describe the work (community information, accountability reporting, civic coverage) rather than the identity. Avoid credentials-based gatekeeping.

3. Fund Infrastructure, Not Just Content

Top needs go beyond money: community (7/9), audience development (6/9), business guidance (5/9), healthcare (5/9). Wrap grants with cohort-based support — peer community, business coaching, and practical resources. This becomes the replicable model for other Press Forward chapters.

4. Build the Collaboration Bridge

8 of 9 are interested in collaborating with other creators. Partnership challenges (editorial control, communication) are structural. Fund both solo creator projects AND creator-newsroom collaborations, but require clear partnership frameworks as part of the application.

5. Address the Revenue Diversification Gap

The bimodal revenue distribution reveals a “sustainability gap.” Grants can bridge creators from side-project/subsistence ($5K–15K) to sustainability ($50K+). Require grantees to develop a revenue diversification plan as part of their funded work.

6. Prioritize Coverage Gaps

Current respondents skew toward food (3) and sports (2). Limited representation in immigration, housing, education, and criminal justice. The grant should incentivize coverage in underserved beats and neighborhoods south and west of downtown.

7. Make It Replicable

Document everything. The April 9 Midwest Press Forwards convening and the April 10 Illinois Local News Summit are opportunities to share this model. Package the survey instrument, toolkit, and grant framework for other chapters to adapt.
